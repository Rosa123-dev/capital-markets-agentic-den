What Your Secretary Can Do

Because your agent is built inside Microsoft 365 Copilot, it has secure access to your real work data — emails, meetings, chats and files. Here's what you can make it do.

Email Triage

Get a prioritised summary of unread emails, flag urgent items, and surface anything that needs a reply today.

Meeting Preparation

Before any meeting, get a briefing — who's attending, what was discussed last time, relevant emails and files.

Teams Catch-Up

Summarise what you missed in Teams channels and chats while you were away or in back-to-back meetings.

Draft Replies

Ask it to draft a response to any email in your tone — then review and send. You stay in control.

Find Anything

Search across emails, files and SharePoint for that document or conversation you can never quite locate.

Summarise Documents

Drop a SharePoint link and ask for a summary, key actions, or a comparison against another document.

The most important part

Writing Your Agent's Instructions

The instructions are what make your agent yours. A great prompt makes the difference between a generic assistant and one that actually works for you.

What a strong prompt includes Do this

  • A clear role — "You are my personal work assistant"
  • Your name and job context so it personalises responses
  • What it should prioritise (e.g. emails from specific people)
  • The tone you want — formal, concise, friendly
  • What it should always do (e.g. end briefs with action items)
  • What it should never do (e.g. send emails without asking)
  • How you want information structured

Common mistakes to avoid Don't do this

  • Being too vague — "be helpful" tells it nothing specific
  • Forgetting to set boundaries on what it can act on
  • Not specifying a tone — it'll default to corporate speak
  • Writing one long paragraph — use sections or bullet points
  • Skipping context about your role
  • Leaving out formatting preferences — you'll get walls of text
Example prompt — copy, edit and make it yours
Full System Prompt
You are my personal AI work secretary. My name is [YOUR NAME] and I work as a [YOUR ROLE] at [YOUR FIRM], focused on [YOUR AREA e.g. Capital Markets clients].

Your job is to help me stay on top of my day. You have access to my Outlook emails, my calendar, my Teams messages, and my SharePoint files. Use all of these to give me accurate, relevant, up-to-date answers.

When I ask for a daily briefing, always structure it as:
1. Urgent emails that need a reply today
2. Meetings today — with a one-line summary of what each is about
3. Any important Teams messages I've missed
4. One thing I should be aware of or follow up on

When preparing me for a meeting:
- Tell me who is attending and their role
- Summarise any relevant recent email threads with those people
- Pull out any documents shared in relation to this meeting
- Suggest 2–3 smart questions I could ask

When drafting emails for me:
- Match my tone: professional but warm, concise, no waffle
- Always show me the draft before sending — never send automatically
- Keep subject lines short and clear

Always respond in bullet points or short paragraphs. Never give me walls of text. If you're unsure about something, say so — don't guess.

Going deeper

Ways to Take It Further

Once your basic agent is working, here are three ways to make it significantly more powerful.

01

Make it time-aware

Tell it to always check the date before responding so its briefings reflect what's actually coming up, not what's already passed.

02

Add VIP rules

Instruct it to always flag emails from specific people — your manager, key clients — as high priority regardless of subject.

03

Give it your writing style

Paste in an example email you've written and say "match this tone when drafting replies for me."

Before you're done

Test Your Agent

Work through these five checks to make sure your agent is working properly. Tick each one off as you go.

01

Morning briefing

Ask: "Give me my morning briefing — what emails need attention today and what meetings do I have?" — it should pull from both Outlook and Calendar and return a structured summary.

02

Meeting preparation

Ask: "Prepare me for my next meeting — who's attending and what should I know going in?" — it should pull attendees, any relevant email threads, and suggest questions to ask.

03

Teams catch-up

Ask: "What did I miss in Teams while I was in meetings this afternoon?" — it should summarise unread messages across your key channels, not just list them.

04

Draft a reply

Ask: "Draft a reply to [person]'s last email — keep it professional and say I'll follow up by end of week." — it should match your tone and show you the draft before sending anything.

05

Find a document

Ask: "Find the latest version of [a real document you know exists] and give me a one-paragraph summary." — it should locate it in SharePoint or OneDrive and summarise the content accurately.
